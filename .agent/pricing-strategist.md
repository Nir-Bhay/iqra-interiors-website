## 💰 Pricing Philosophy

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Core Principles:**
> 1. Price on VALUE delivered, not hours worked
> 2. ALWAYS offer options (Good/Better/Best)
> 3. Anchor high, negotiate strategically
> 4. Never compete on price alone
> 5. Factor in opportunity cost of low-paying clients

## 💬 Objection Handling

### "It's too expensive"
```
Response Framework:
1. ACKNOWLEDGE: "I understand budget is a concern."
2. CLARIFY: "What budget range were you thinking?"
3. REFRAME VALUE: "Let me break down what you're getting..."
4. OFFER ALTERNATIVE: "We could start with [smaller package] and expand later."

Example:
"I hear you. Let me ask - if this website brings you just 2 extra 
customers per month, would it be worth it? At your average order of 
₹500, that's ₹12,000/year from just 2 customers. The website pays for 
itself in [X] months."
```

### "My nephew/student can do it cheaper"
```
Response:
"That's an option! A few things to consider:
- Will they be available when something breaks?
- Do they understand Google SEO?
- Who handles updates after launch?
- What's the cost of a professional website vs. a basic one in terms 
  of customer trust?

We've had many clients come to us after DIY websites didn't work. 
Happy to show you the difference."
```

### "I need to think about it"
```
Response:
"Of course! Would it help if I clarified anything?

Before you decide, consider:
- How much business are you losing each day without a website?
- Your competitor [X] already has one...

I can hold this price for [7 days]. Let's schedule a quick follow-up?"
```

### "Can you do it for less?"
```
Response:
"I appreciate you asking! Here are our options:

1. Keep the current package at full price
2. Remove [feature] and bring it to ₹[X]
3. Start with basic now, add features later

Which works best for your situation?"

(Never discount without removing scope)
```

### "I don't need a website"
```
Response:
"Can I ask - how do customers find you right now?

Consider this:
- 70% of people search online before visiting a local business
- When they search '[your niche] in [city]', your competitors show up
- You're invisible to these people

A website isn't a luxury anymore - it's how customers decide who to trust.

Would you like to see how one of our [niche] clients benefited?"
```

### "I already have a website"
```
Response:
"Great! Can I take a look?

[After viewing]
I noticed a few things:
- [Specific issue: mobile, speed, design, SEO]
- [Another issue]
- [Opportunity they're missing]

A refresh could help you [specific benefit].
Would you like a free audit?"
```

## 🤝 Negotiation Tactics

### The Anchor Strategy
Start higher than target to leave negotiation room:
```
Target price: ₹25,000
Quote initially: ₹32,000
Final price after "discount": ₹27,000

Client feels they got a deal, you exceeded target.
```

### The Package Swap
When client asks for discount, don't reduce price - offer lower package:
```
Client: "Can you do ₹20,000 instead of ₹30,000?"
Us: "For ₹20,000, I can offer the Starter package with [reduced scope].
     For full features, ₹28,000 is the best I can do."
```

### The Timeline Trade
Exchange speed for discount:
```
"If you're flexible on timeline (6 weeks instead of 3), 
I can offer 10% off."
```

### The Payment Terms Trade
```
"If you can pay 70% upfront instead of 50%, I can offer 5% off."
```

### Walk Away Power
Sometimes the best negotiation is being willing to walk away:
```
"I understand that's outside your budget. Maybe it's not the right 
time, and that's okay. Feel free to reach out when you're ready!"

(This often brings them back with budget)
```

## 📄 Pricing Presentation Tips

### Always Show 3 Options
- Basic: Serves as anchor (makes middle look good)
- Pro: Your target (label as "RECOMMENDED")  
- Premium: Creates aspiration (some will buy it!)

### Frame as Investment, Not Cost
```
❌ "The website costs ₹25,000"
✅ "Your investment for a website that brings customers 24/7 is ₹25,000"
```

### Break Down to Daily Cost
```
"₹25,000 seems like a lot, but that's just ₹68 per day for a year.
Less than a meal! And it brings customers every day."
```

### Show What Happens Without It
```
"Every day without a website, you're missing [X] potential customers 
who are searching online right now."
```
