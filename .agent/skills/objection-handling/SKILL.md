# Objection Handling Skill

> **Purpose:** Turn objections into opportunities by providing effective responses to common client concerns

---

## 🎯 When to Use

Use this skill when:
- Lead raises concerns about price
- Lead is hesitant to move forward
- Lead mentions competitors or alternatives
- Need to overcome skepticism

---

## 🧠 Objection Handling Philosophy

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Key Principle:** Objections are NOT rejections.
> 
> They are:
> - Requests for more information
> - Signs of interest (they're thinking about it!)
> - Opportunities to build trust
> 
> **The LAER Framework:**
> 1. **L**isten - Let them fully express the concern
> 2. **A**cknowledge - Show you understand
> 3. **E**xplore - Ask clarifying questions
> 4. **R**espond - Address with relevant solution

---

## 💰 Price Objections

### "It's too expensive"
```markdown
## Response Framework

**Acknowledge:**
"I understand budget is a concern for most businesses."

**Explore:**
"Can I ask what budget you had in mind?"
"What are you comparing our pricing to?"

**Respond (Options):**

Option A - Break Down Value:
"Let me break it down: For ₹25,000, you get a website that works 
24/7 for your business. That's ₹68 per day for a year. Less than 
a meal, and it brings customers every day."

Option B - ROI Focus:
"If this website brings you just 2 extra customers per month at 
your average order of ₹500, it pays for itself in [X] months. 
How many customers would make it worth it for you?"

Option C - Offer Alternatives:
"I can offer a few options:
1. Start with our Basic package at ₹15,000
2. Split payment into 3 installments
3. Remove [feature] to bring it to ₹[X]
Which would work best?"
```

### "I can get it cheaper elsewhere"
```markdown
**Acknowledge:**
"You're right, there are cheaper options out there."

**Explore:**
"What have you found? I'd love to understand what you're comparing."

**Respond:**
"The difference usually comes down to:
- Quality: Will it look professional or template-y?
- Support: Who helps when something breaks?
- Results: Will it actually bring customers?

We've had many clients come to us after a cheap website didn't 
work. I'd rather do it right the first time. Would you like to 
see examples of the difference?"
```

### "I don't have budget right now"
```markdown
**Acknowledge:**
"I completely understand. Cash flow is real."

**Explore:**
"Is it a matter of timing, or the overall amount?"

**Respond:**

If timing:
"When would be a better time to revisit this? I can check in 
with you in [X] weeks."

If amount:
"We could start with something smaller now and expand later. 
Or, I can offer a payment plan - 50% now, 50% after launch?"
```

## 🤔 Need/Value Objections

### "I don't need a website"
```markdown
**Acknowledge:**
"I hear that from many business owners."

**Explore:**
"Can I ask - how do customers find you right now?"

**Respond:**
"Here's what's happening:
- 70% of people search online before visiting a local business
- When they search '[your niche] in [city]', your competitors 
  with websites show up. You don't.
- These are people actively looking to spend money

It's not about whether YOU think you need a website. It's about 
whether your CUSTOMERS expect you to have one. Would you like to 
see what comes up when I search for your niche?"
```

### "My nephew/student can make me a website"
```markdown
**Acknowledge:**
"That's definitely an option!"

**Explore:**
"Has he made websites for businesses before?"

**Respond:**
"A few things to consider:
1. Availability: What happens when something breaks and he's 
   busy with school/job?
2. Expertise: Does he know SEO to get you found on Google?
3. Professional look: Will it look like a business website or 
   a student project?
4. Updates: Who handles changes after 6 months?

We've had many clients come to us after DIY attempts didn't 
give them results. Happy to show you the difference - no 
pressure, just so you can compare."
```

### "I already have a website"
```markdown
**Acknowledge:**
"That's great that you're already online!"

**Explore:**
"Can I take a quick look at it?"

[After viewing]

**Respond:**
"I noticed a few things:
- [Specific issue: Not mobile-friendly?]
- [Specific issue: Slow loading?]
- [Specific issue: Old design?]
- [Specific issue: Not ranking on Google?]

These might be costing you customers. Would you like a free 
audit? I'll share 3 specific improvements - no strings attached."
```

### "Social media is enough for me"
```markdown
**Acknowledge:**
"Social media is definitely important!"

**Explore:**
"How much business comes from social vs. other sources?"

**Respond:**
"Here's the thing about social:
- You don't OWN it - algorithms change, accounts get hacked
- Not everyone is on Instagram (especially older customers)
- When someone searches Google, they find websites, not posts
- A website makes you look more professional and trustworthy

The best businesses use BOTH. Your social brings people IN, 
your website CONVERTS them. Want to see how this works?"
```

## ⏰ Timing/Decision Objections

### "I need to think about it"
```markdown
**Acknowledge:**
"Absolutely, this is an important decision."

**Explore:**
"Of course! Is there anything specific you're unsure about that 
I could help clarify?"

**Respond:**

If they share concern:
[Address that specific concern]

If they don't:
"I completely understand. Here's what I'd ask you to consider:
- Every day without a website, potential customers are finding 
  your competitors instead
- [Competitor name] is already ranking for [keyword]

I can hold this quote for 7 days. Can I check in with you 
next [day]?"
```

### "I need to discuss with my partner/family"
```markdown
**Acknowledge:**
"That makes total sense - it's an important business decision."

**Explore:**
"What questions do you think they might have?"

**Respond:**
"Would it help if I prepared a quick summary you could share 
with them? I can highlight:
- What we'll build
- The investment
- Expected results
- Examples of similar projects

That way, the conversation is clear. Should I send that over?"
```

### "This isn't a good time"
```markdown
**Acknowledge:**
"I completely understand, especially with [season/situation]."

**Explore:**
"When would be a better time to revisit this?"

**Respond:**
"No problem! A few things to think about:
- Now might actually be the best time since [reason]
- Getting the website ready before [busy season] means you 
  capture more business when it matters

But I respect your timeline. Should I check in [specific time]?"
```

## 🆚 Competitor/Alternative Objections

### "I'm looking at other options"
```markdown
**Acknowledge:**
"That's smart - you should compare."

**Explore:**
"What other options are you considering?"

**Respond:**
"Here's what sets us apart:
- [Point 1 relevant to their situation]
- [Point 2]
- [Point 3]

Would it help if I did a quick comparison? I want you to make 
the best choice for [Business Name], even if it's not us."
```

### "I'll just use Wix/WordPress myself"
```markdown
**Acknowledge:**
"Those are popular options!"

**Explore:**
"Have you tried building a website before?"

**Respond:**
"DIY platforms seem easy, but here's what I've seen:
- Looks: They often look template-y, not custom
- Time: Most business owners give up halfway
- SEO: Getting Google ranking is harder than it looks
- Cost: Wix premium + domain + plugins adds up

Our clients tried DIY first. They came to us because they 
wanted something that WORKS, not just something that exists.

The question is: Is your time worth ₹[X] per hour? If yes, 
hiring us is actually cheaper than doing it yourself."
```

## 🔴 Hard Objections

### "I had a bad experience with developers before"
```markdown
**Acknowledge:**
"I'm really sorry to hear that. Unfortunately, many businesses 
have been burned by unprofessional developers."

**Explore:**
"What happened, if you don't mind sharing?"

**Respond:**
"That's exactly why we do things differently:
- Clear contract with milestones
- You see designs BEFORE development begins
- Payment tied to deliverables
- 30-day support after launch included
- References from similar businesses available

I can connect you with a client who had similar concerns. 
Would that help?"
```

### "I don't trust online payments"
```markdown
**Respond:**
"Completely understandable!

We offer multiple options:
- Bank transfer (most businesses prefer this)
- Cash payment (we can meet in person)
- UPI payment
- Cheque

We also provide a proper invoice and receipt for everything. 
What payment method works best for you?"
```
