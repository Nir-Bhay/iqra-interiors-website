# ICP Builder Skill

> **Purpose:** Create detailed Ideal Customer Profiles that help identify and prioritize the best-fit leads

---

## 🎯 When to Use

Use this skill when:
- Defining who to target for a niche
- Creating lead scoring criteria
- Qualifying leads before outreach
- Refining targeting based on results

---

## 📋 ICP Template

### Full ICP Structure
```markdown
# Ideal Customer Profile: [Niche Name]

## Overview
**Target:** [1-sentence description]
**Perfect Fit Score:** [What makes someone 100% fit]
**Deal Size Range:** ₹[X] - ₹[Y]

## Demographics
| Attribute | Ideal | Acceptable | Avoid |
|-----------|-------|------------|-------|
| Business Age | 3-15 years | 1-3 or 15+ years | <1 year |
| Employee Count | 5-50 | 2-5 or 50-100 | 1 or 100+ |
| Annual Revenue | ₹20L-2Cr | ₹10-20L or 2-10Cr | <₹10L or >₹10Cr |
| Location | Tier 1 & 2 cities | Tier 3 cities | Rural |

## Firmographics
| Attribute | Ideal | Acceptable | Avoid |
|-----------|-------|------------|-------|
| Current Website | None or 5+ years old | 2-5 years old | New (< 1 year) |
| Google Rating | 4.0+ stars | 3.5-4.0 | <3.5 |
| Review Count | 50+ | 20-50 | <20 |
| Social Presence | Active Instagram | Has profiles | None at all |

## Psychographics
| Trait | Indicators |
|-------|------------|
| Growth Mindset | Expanding, hiring, investing in ads |
| Values Quality | Premium pricing, quality reviews |
| Decision Ready | Recently searched for web services |
| Budget Available | Profitable, established |

## Pain Points (Why They Need Us)
1. **Primary Pain:** [Main problem we solve]
2. **Secondary Pain:** [Supporting problem]
3. **Trigger Event:** [What makes them buy NOW]

## Objections to Expect
1. [Objection 1] → [How to handle]
2. [Objection 2] → [How to handle]

## Where to Find Them
- **Online:** [Google Maps, JustDial, LinkedIn, etc.]
- **Offline:** [Events, associations, etc.]
- **Referral Source:** [Who refers them]

## Sample Profile Description
> "[Business Name] is a [X]-year-old [niche] in [city] with [Y] Google 
> reviews (Z stars). They have [website status] but are active on 
> [social platforms] with [follower count]. Signs of growth: [evidence]. 
> Decision maker: [role]. Estimated budget: ₹[X]."
```

## 🔢 Lead Scoring Matrix

### Scoring Criteria
```markdown
## Lead Score Calculation

### Fit Score (0-50 points)
| Criteria | Points | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------|
| In target niche | 0-10 | Exact match = 10 |
| In target location | 0-10 | Tier 1 city = 10 |
| Right business size | 0-10 | 5-50 employees = 10 |
| Budget indicators | 0-10 | Premium pricing = 10 |
| Decision maker reachable | 0-10 | Direct contact = 10 |

### Need Score (0-30 points)
| Criteria | Points | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------|
| No website | 20 | Clear need |
| Outdated website (5+ yrs) | 15 | Likely need |
| Poor mobile experience | 10 | Needs update |
| Competitor has better site | 10 | Competitive pressure |
| Active advertising | 10 | Spending on marketing |

### Engagement Score (0-20 points)
| Criteria | Points | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Responded to outreach | 10 | Interested |
| Asked questions | 5 | Engaged |
| Requested proposal | 10 | Ready to buy |
| Referred by client | 10 | Warm lead |

### Total Score Interpretation
| Score | Tier | Action |
|-------|------|--------|
| 80-100 | A (Hot) | Prioritize, personalize heavily |
| 60-79 | B (Warm) | Standard outreach sequence |
| 40-59 | C (Cool) | Nurture, low priority |
| 0-39 | D (Cold) | Skip or save |
```

#### Persona 1: Restaurateur Raj
```
## Persona: Restaurateur Raj

### Profile
**Age:** 35-50
**Role:** Owner
**Business:** Restaurant/Cafe (3-10 years old)

### Background
- Started from scratch, built reputation on food quality
- Has loyal customer base but limited walk-in growth
- Active on Instagram (posting food pics)
- Sees competitors getting online orders

### Goals
- Get more customers during off-peak hours
- Compete with Zomato/Swiggy without commission
- Build direct customer relationships
- Look more professional

### Challenges
- Not tech-savvy, scared of technology
- Busy running restaurant, no time
- Had bad experience with cheap developer
- Thinks websites are expensive

### Messaging That Works
- Lead with: "Get online orders without Swiggy's 25% cut"
- Emphasize: Simplicity, we handle everything
- Prove with: Restaurant case study with order numbers
- Avoid: Technical jargon
```

## 📊 Qualification Questions

### Discovery Call Questions
```markdown
## Lead Qualification Framework

### Budget Qualification
- "What budget range have you set aside for this?"
- "Have you invested in marketing tools before?"
- "Is this something you'd want to move on quickly?"

### Need Qualification
- "What's driving your interest in a website right now?"
- "What's not working with your current online presence?"
- "What would success look like for you?"

### Timeline Qualification
- "When would you ideally want to launch?"
- "Is there an event or deadline driving this?"
- "How quickly can you gather content/photos?"

### Decision Qualification
- "Who else is involved in this decision?"
- "What would the approval process look like?"
- "Have you looked at other options?"

### Red Flag Questions
- "What made you reach out to us specifically?"
- "What's your biggest concern about this project?"
- "What would make you NOT move forward?"
```
